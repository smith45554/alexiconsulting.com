I am a Program Support Specialist at the U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service, building the tools and governance frameworks that help federal employees work smarter with AI. I founded FWS’s first multi-region AI Collaboration Hub and have spent the past several years at the intersection of public service, technology modernization, and responsible AI deployment.

Before FWS, I served as a Federal Communications Consultant for the National Park Service Office of Native American Affairs, supported climate change education at the U.S. Forest Service, and spent three years as an Interpretive Park Ranger in Washington, D.C. Earlier roles include an AmeriCorps RARE fellowship in Eastern Oregon and health policy research at the Schuyler Center for Analysis and Advocacy in Albany, NY.

I hold an MPH in Health Policy from The George Washington University and a BA in Public Health (with honors) from the University of South Carolina, where I was named an Outstanding Senior. I am currently completing an MPA in Public Management at Indiana University’s O’Neill School of Public & Environmental Affairs (Expected 2027).

Outside of work, I enjoy spending time on public lands and hiking, international travel, horses and equine therapy, and environmental conservation.
