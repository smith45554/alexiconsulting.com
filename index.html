Yann Dubois

PhD student in Machine Learning

yanndubois96@gmail.com GitHub Twitter LinkedIn Google Scholar

I am a researcher at OpenAI, where I co-lead the Post-training Frontiers team with Eric Mitchell. Our team focuses on training and deploying models that are maximally useful for productive and professional use cases (e.g., o1, o3, GPT-5 Thinking, etc.).

Previously, I was a PhD student in machine learning at Stanford University, co-advised by Percy Liang and Tatsu Hashimoto, and funded by a Knights-Hennessy Scholarship. Before that I traveled around and did some research at the Vector Institute, completed my AI Residency at Facebook AI, and worked in different startups including Grab where I was designing algorithms to analyze text in under-research languages (Thai, Khmer, Burmese, …) before LLMs were a thing.

Outside of work, I enjoy exercising with friends, traveling, and studying geopolitics.

Note: Please do not contact me about quant jobs; I’m not interested regardless of compensation.
